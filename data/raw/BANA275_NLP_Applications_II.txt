Keyword & TF-IDF Recommendations
Content-based filtering using text features
11
How it works:
Extract keywords from product descriptions or reviews
Compute TF-IDF vectors for each item
Recommend items with highest cosine similarity
Strengths:
Simple, interpretable, fast, no training data needed
Great cold-start fallback in production systems
Limitation:
No semantic understanding — "running shoe" won't match "jogging sneaker"

Word2Vec / GloVe for Item Representations
Treating interaction sequences as sentences

Treat user interaction history as a "sentence" where items are "words"

Spotify: Song sequences → song embeddings for playlist generation

Q&A Taxonomy
37

Extractive
Select a span from the source text as the answer
"The capital of France is Paris"

Abstractive
Generate a new answer by synthesizing information
Produces novel text not found verbatim in source

Generative (LLM)
Generate answers from parametric knowledge or retrieved context
Most flexible but requires faithfulness controls

Also consider: Open vs. Closed domain  |  Single-hop vs. Multi-hop reasoning
Closed-Book Q&A
LLM answers from its parametric knowledge alone
38

User: "What year was the Eiffel Tower built?"
LLM: "The Eiffel Tower was completed in 1889."
When it works:
General knowledge, well-known facts, simple lookups
When it fails:
Domain-specific questions, recent information, company policies
Anything requiring citations or verifiability
Hallucination risk is high and undetectable

The Long-Context Challenge
What happens when the document exceeds the context window?
54

Chunking
Split document into overlapping chunks, summarize each, combine
Pro: Simple  |  Con: Loses cross-section connections

Map-Reduce
Summarize each chunk independently, then summarize the summaries
Pro: Scalable  |  Con: Information loss at each stage

Hierarchical
Build summary tree: paragraphs → sections → document
Pro: Preserves structure  |  Con: More complex, higher cost
55
Map-Reduce Summarization Strategy

Faithfulness: The Core Risk
56

Original: "Revenue grew 12% to $4.2B, driven by cloud services."
Faithful: "Revenue increased 12% to $4.2 billion, led by cloud growth."
Unfaithful: "Revenue surged 15% to $4.5B, driven by AI product launches."
Why it happens:
LLMs optimize for fluency, not factual accuracy
Numbers, dates, and names are especially vulnerable
Longer documents = more room for drift from source material

Summarization Metrics
58
ROUGE
N-gram overlap with reference summaries. Simple but misses semantic quality.
High ROUGE ≠ good summary
BERTScore
Semantic similarity using contextual embeddings. Better than ROUGE.
Doesn't catch factual errors
Factual Consistency
Does the summary only state facts present in the source?
Hard to automate reliably
Human Evaluation
Gold standard: fluency, coherence, informativeness, faithfulness
Expensive, slow, subjective
59
A fluent summary that's unfaithful
is worse than no summary at all.
Always verify: numbers, names, dates, and causal claims
